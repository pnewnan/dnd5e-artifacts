What Aurelia Knows

Aurelia knows Eric is Sarynthia's covert contact in Hollowgate. He sends monthly intelligence reports. Recently his messages became more frequent and alarmed — then stopped entirely.

From His Last Reports

  • Border villages in Fastingbreak experiencing food shortages
  • Elkenquay levying very high export taxes on goods destined for Fastingbreak
  • Shipments becoming smaller, delayed, or unprofitable
  • Entire convoys failing to depart or arrive — cause unknown
  • Eric believed something larger was developing
  • His correspondence has now stopped entirely.

Princess Insight Prompts

Would Elkenquay realistically extort trade shipments from Fastingbreak?
WIS 15+

It smells like propaganda. If stories about "extortion taxes" are spreading, someone benefits from people believing Elkenquay is the villain. Who benefits from that anger?

Why would people sent to gather information stop returning?
WIS 15+

Someone powerful is likely silencing questions. Could be:

  • A faction inside Fastingbreak
  • Agents from Elkenquay
  • A third party benefiting from the confusion
If tensions are rising, what is the most likely cause?
WIS 15+

Tensions often grow not from direct conflict but manipulation. A rival power, merchant consortium, or hidden faction may be pushing both sides toward hostility.

What strategic advantage would Elkenquay gain from harming Fastingbreak economically?
INT 10

Weakening Fastingbreak could help Elkenquay dominate regional trade.

If the tariffs are real, how might they be happening?
INT 10

Elkenquay could simply have increased border taxes through normal administrative channels.

1. Sealed Letter — To the Office of Trade Observations

Contents

I regret that I must accelerate my previous report. Matters here are increasingly irregular.

Over the past several weeks I have observed inconsistencies in caravan manifests and border taxes. Several shipments expected to pass through have failed to arrive, though no official notice of delay has been issued.

Several merchants have quietly complained that export taxes have risen to levels that would make trade unsustainable. Selective tariffs have increased again.

I hesitate to speculate, but these actions could create significant strain between our nations should they continue. I intend to investigate further.

"If I am correct, the disruptions are not merely trade disputes. Someone is manipulating supply routes deliberately."

Missing Convoys — Investigation Arc
Kobolds · The crate · Eric's body · Regional conspiracy
▼
The Surface Problem

Multiple supply convoys missing near Hollowgate. Food shortages, rising prices, nervous travelers. The mayor offers 400 gp for resolution. Feels like a kobold bandit problem at first.

What Actually Happened

A human agent hired kobolds to retrieve a specific crate from one convoy. The kobolds took the other wagons because they could. The employer is now dying in the kobold den after being mauled by their pet.

The crate contains an old book tied to the deeper corruption storyline.
Key NPCs
  • Robyn — timid kobold outcast, sympathetic, can guide party to caverns
  • The Chieftain — unexpectedly charismatic, self-declared king, offers "fight the pet" deal
  • The Dying Human — Elkenquay-associated, mauled, connects to regional conspiracy
The Turning Point

While escaping the caverns, the party finds Eric Huwithakay's body. This transitions the arc from "kobold problem" to "larger conspiracy." His satchel contents are all here.

Why the Carnival Matters

The Moonlit Mirage Carnival arrives in Hollowgate during the same period as the convoy disruptions. It is suffering from the same missing supply convoys — and the same tunnels. Its problems and the town's problems are the same problem.

  • The carnival's missing food convoys were diverted by the same kobolds
  • Recovering the carnival's convoy and the mayor's missing convoy may be the same objective
  • The 400 gp reward from Jeffrey and the mayor's reward may both apply
Phase One — "We Need Food"

The carnival arrives subdued and struggling. Chaelynn the ringmaster needs help. The party's investigation of the kobold tunnels restores the carnival's supplies — and enables the full nighttime show.

The party's success in Phase One triggers Phase Two.
Phase Two — The Pallor & The Artifact

Once the full show opens, a modified artifact at the carnival's center begins feeding on crowd emotion and accelerating parasitic growth inside afflicted performers. The Nightbloom — an unlisted fire act added to celebrate the convoy's return — is when the outbreak occurs.

  • The artifact was recently modified — someone inserted a parasitic object inside it
  • The artifact itself is innocent; what was done to it is not
  • Destroying the artifact kills all creatures instantly
  • The modification may be connected to the same faction behind the convoy disruptions
Key NPCs
  • Chaelynn — ringmaster, protective of her people, will resist interference with the artifact until she sees the truth
  • Magnus Eillin — sideshow storyteller, notices something wrong during Act 1, points the party toward the artifact

The Road to Dunmore — Departure Hook
Farm burnings · Relief convoy · Escort needed · The Hollow Road
▼

News Arriving in Hollowgate

What People Are Saying

Word has reached Hollowgate in the past day or two — farmers and travelers coming in from the east, many of them on foot and carrying little. Farms along the road toward Dunmore have been burned. The accounts vary, but the shape of the story is consistent: something is attacking settlements on the Hollow Road, and people are not staying to find out what.

  • At least two farmsteads confirmed burned, others abandoned
  • Refugees arriving with no wagons, no food, burned-smell on their clothes
  • Travelers report claw marks on buildings — large ones
  • No one has seen the attackers clearly. Accounts mention movement at night, fire, and screaming.
  • The road to Dunmore is now considered dangerous — most merchants have stopped using it
The Supply Problem

Dunmore is a city of ten thousand people. The road disruption is not an abstract concern — it is the primary supply artery connecting Hollowgate and the surrounding region to Dunmore. With that route compromised, shortages that were already beginning will accelerate sharply within days.

"The convoys from the east stopped coming three days ago. If we don't get something through soon, Dunmore starts rationing."

The Convoy & The Ask

Mara Vell — Wagon Leader

A local organizer named Mara Vell has been quietly pulling together a relief convoy: grain, medical supplies, tools, and a handful of civilian volunteers willing to make the run. She is practical and exhausted and not asking for heroes — she is asking for capable people willing to walk beside wagons that need to get through.

  • The convoy is small by necessity — fewer wagons means less of a target
  • The cargo is essential, not valuable — no one is getting rich off grain and bandages
  • She has heard the party handled themselves well during the carnival incident
  • She offers what she can: a share of whatever goodwill Dunmore extends, and passage there
"I don't need soldiers. I need people who won't run when something goes wrong."
Who Else Is Coming

A small group has already committed to the convoy regardless of danger — refugees with nowhere else to go, an elderly farmer named Old Thom who refuses to leave his salvaged tools, and a pair of siblings separated from their parents during one of the farm attacks. They are the human face of why the convoy matters.

What the GM Knows

The Truth Beneath the Reports

The claw marks are not what they seem. The road has genuine dangers — isolated bandits, frightened people making bad decisions — but the organized threat the reports describe is manufactured. Someone fed a false account of creature attacks to the local Ashfall Watch, who set up a blockade under Commander Durgan Valeforge citing road security. The blockade is the real problem. The claw marks were staged.

Beneath that: a local landowner has been quietly purchasing supply contracts and rerouting goods away from smaller villages toward Dunmore's wealthier districts at a profit. The chaos on the road is cover that happens to serve him perfectly — whether he arranged any of it or simply recognized an opportunity is a question the party may investigate.

The party will find a blockade, not a monster. The monster story is the thing worth pulling apart.

GM Conclusion — Active Threads in Hollowgate

  • Eric Huwithakay was a Sarynthian intelligence contact investigating convoy disruptions, suspicious tariffs, and regional instability. He is dead. His investigation is not over.
  • The kobold tunnels connect three threads: Eric's investigation, the missing trade convoys, and the Moonlit Mirage Carnival's supply shortage. They are the same problem.
  • A human agent wearing Elkenquay colors hired the kobolds. He is dying in their den. His presence connects the local disruption to the Elkenquay/Fastingbreak political conspiracy.
  • Vanya's mission to silence the current gate scribe runs in parallel to everything else. Her timeline and the party's may collide.
  • The carnival artifact was modified recently — by whom, and whether it connects to the convoy diversion, is an open question that points beyond Hollowgate.
  • Princess Aurelia's intelligence points the same direction as Eric's investigation from the other side. They are closing in on the same answer.
  • The Hollow Road to Dunmore is reported as dangerous — farm burnings, creature attacks, refugees arriving on foot. The true cause is a staged blockade, an opportunist landowner, and a false threat report fed to the local militia. The party is about to escort a relief convoy into that tangle.
