Moonlit Mirage Carnival

Prize Counter & Curiosities

⁂

The Moonlit Mirage travels between isolated villages, crossroads settlements, trade towns, and major cities. Most games offer small novelty prizes worth only a few copper — but scattered among the wagons and locked display cases are genuine curiosities, unusual artifacts, and rare oddities that attract collectors, thieves, and adventurers alike.

Locked Display Cases

Mid-Tier Curiosities

Not ordinary prizes. Most are displayed behind counters where collectors and wealthier travelers can admire them.

Preserved Six-Winged Raven 8 gp

Mounted beneath a warped glass dome atop a black wooden base, the raven's feathers shimmer oil-slick blue in lantern light. Its six folded wings are arranged unnaturally neatly, and its eyes always appear wet, as though the creature died moments ago. Carnival rumor claims it was found frozen inside a glacier far to the north.

Minor Effect

Once per day, the owner may spend one uninterrupted minute studying the raven before sleep. That night, they gain unusually vivid dreams. The next morning, they gain advantage on one Insight or Perception check related to noticing hidden danger, betrayal, or deception.

The raven itself never moves. Probably.

Dwarven Ale Stein of Echoing Toasts 12 gp

This heavy stoneware tankard is decorated with chipped enamel scenes of drunken dwarves dancing around a bonfire. When filled and raised in celebration, the mug softly repeats the last triumphant phrase ever shouted into it. The echo is faint, distant, and often slightly delayed.

Minor Effect

Once per long rest, after making a heartfelt toast or rallying speech while drinking from the stein, the owner and one nearby ally both gain +1 on their next saving throw against fear effects.

The benefit fades after one hour if unused.

Folding Brass Spyglass 15 gp

An old collapsible spyglass etched with faded constellations and tiny moons along the brass tubing. Though worn and dented, the lenses remain surprisingly clear. A small engraving near the eyepiece reads: "The horizon remembers."

Minor Effect

While observing distant terrain, creatures, ships, roads, or structures for at least one minute, the user gains advantage on checks related to spotting ambushes, hidden movement, or distant landmarks.

Once per day, the user may also reroll a failed Survival check made for navigation.

Sapphire Glass Music Box 18 gp

A delicate hand-cranked music box crafted from blue-tinted glass panels reinforced with silver filigree. It plays a haunting carnival melody that seems familiar even to those hearing it for the first time. One note is permanently missing. Listeners often find themselves unconsciously waiting for it.

Minor Effect

When opened and played during a short rest, up to three listeners gain advantage on their next saving throw against being frightened or emotionally manipulated. The effect lasts until the next dawn.

The mechanism is fragile. Only 10 plays remain before the spring finally fails. Each use produces slightly more distortion in the melody — as though the song itself is slowly being forgotten.

Mask of the Laughing Court 20 gp

A lacquered porcelain mask from a distant noble theater. The smiling expression changes subtly depending on the angle of the light. Some swear the mask reflects the wearer's true mood rather than the expression painted upon it.

Minor Effect

While worn during a conversation, performance, or social deception, the wearer gains advantage on one Deception or Performance check per day.

After removing the mask, the wearer experiences a brief moment of emotional disorientation — as though they momentarily forgot which emotions were truly theirs.

Objects That Make Adventurers Stop Walking

Big Ticket Items

The party likely cannot afford them yet — ideal for bargains, favors, performances, thefts, or future goals.

The Starfall Feather Cloak 75 gp

A midnight-blue feathered traveling cloak stitched with reflective silver thread. The feathers subtly shimmer like distant stars whenever exposed to moonlight. The interior lining is impossibly soft and surprisingly warm. Rumor claims the feathers came from a sky-creature that once flew above the clouds.

Minor Effect

While worn outdoors at night, the wearer gains advantage on checks to resist cold weather and advantage on one Acrobatics check per night involving jumping, falling, gliding, or maintaining balance.

For winged creatures, the cloak creates a dramatic silhouette while airborne — the silver-threaded feathers resembling a moving night sky.

The Moonglass Harp 120 gp

A compact lap harp crafted from pale translucent crystal that softly glows beneath moonlight. The strings emit silver light while played, illuminating the musician's hands. The instrument produces unnaturally clear tones that seem to linger in the air a moment too long.

Minor Effect

A bard or musician performing with the harp gains advantage on one Performance check per day. Once per night, the harp may be played softly during a rest — those listening gain advantage on one saving throw against fear or despair before the next dawn.

The Whispering Compass 90 gp

An ornate brass compass suspended within a glass-faced case. Its needle never points north. Instead, it slowly rotates toward places, people, or objects tied to the holder's strongest immediate desire. The compass is frustratingly imprecise — it does not distinguish between greed, fear, obsession, love, or curiosity.

Minor Effect

Once per day, while actively searching for a hidden object, specific person, exit, or desired location, the holder may consult the compass. The GM may provide a vague directional instinct, symbolic clue, emotional impression, or subtle narrative hint.

The compass cannot identify exact locations. Sometimes the needle spins when near powerful magic or emotional turmoil.

The Griffon Rider's Bridle 140 gp

Decorative ceremonial tack woven from silver-thread leather and adorned with polished skybird feathers. Though designed for a griffon, it appears usable for other intelligent flying beasts. The leather remains impossibly supple despite its age. The Feathered Prince becomes visibly agitated whenever the bridle is nearby.

Minor Effect

While openly carrying or displaying the bridle, the owner gains advantage on Animal Handling checks involving birds or flying creatures. Once per day, the owner may calm a panicked flying creature by speaking softly while holding the bridle.

The effect does not magically control creatures, but it can briefly suppress panic or aggression.

The Marionette Noble 110 gp

An exquisitely crafted puppet dressed like forgotten royalty. Its porcelain face is flawless. Its velvet clothing is untouched by dust. Its eyes appear to track movement almost imperceptibly. Nobody within the carnival admits to making it.

Minor Effect

A puppeteer, performer, or storyteller using the marionette gains advantage on one Performance or Persuasion check per day involving storytelling, distraction, or manipulation.

In darkness, the puppet occasionally shifts position slightly when unobserved. It never moves while directly watched. Probably.

The Gilded Egg 250 gp

A fist-sized black egg veined in delicate gold leaf and displayed inside a locked velvet case. The shell feels faintly warm. Sometimes the gold veining appears to pulse beneath lantern light. The carnival owner refuses to explain where it came from. Several performers become visibly uncomfortable when asked about it.

Minor Effect

While carrying the egg during sleep, the owner experiences intense symbolic dreams. Once per week, after sleeping beside the egg, the owner may ask the GM one cryptic question regarding the future, hidden danger, or an important choice. The answer arrives as dream imagery rather than direct words.

Animals react strangely around the egg — some avoid it entirely, others stare at it for extended periods.

The deeper visitors travel into the carnival, the stranger the prizes become.
Near the entrance: candy, ribbons, toys, whistles, masks, playful trinkets.
Deeper in the Big Top: mirrors, preserved insects, bone carvings, fortune slips,
dim reflections, old-looking objects, unsettling dolls — curiosities that seem older than the carnival itself.

The change is subtle enough that most guests never consciously notice it.
