The Setup

The Moonlit Mirage Carnival arrives near Hollowgate during a period of regional instability. To the townsfolk it represents wonder and distraction. To the party it first appears to be a struggling traveling show suffering from the same problems affecting the rest of the region.

Initial Carnival State
  • Subdued, exhausted, partially operational
  • Strangely melancholic beneath its cheerful exterior
  • The nighttime show has not yet begun
  • Many attractions remain closed; lanterns dimmed to conserve oil
  • Food stalls sparse, several performers unavailable
"Once the convoy arrives, the true show can begin." — Chaelynn

The Central Twist

The Players Enable the Horror

The party's success in saving the carnival — recovering the convoy, restoring supplies — is what enables Phase Two. Without a full crowd, the artifact's feeding cycle remains incomplete.

The final tragedy: the creatures matured rapidly because the party succeeded.

Arc Phases

Phase One — "We Need Food"

Grounds the story in practical problems. Missing convoys, kobolds, tunnels. Establishes trust with carnival NPCs.

Phase Two — The Pallor

Slowly transforms the carnival into escalating supernatural horror. Triggered by Phase One success.

Chaelynn — Ringmaster

Who She Is

Joined as a performer years ago, became the person responsible for keeping the carnival alive. Charismatic, capable, fiercely protective of her people, and increasingly overwhelmed by the carnival's deteriorating condition.

What She Reveals — Initially
  • Missing supplies and exhausted workers
  • Postponed performances and morale concerns
What She Hides — Until Trust Is Earned

Her deeper fears about the pallor and the strange nighttime symptoms among workers. She will not reveal these without significant trust from the party.

Phase Two — Her Reaction

When the party investigates the artifact, Chaelynn's instinct is to protect it — it has been the carnival's most prized centerpiece for years and she has never seen it cause harm before. She will resist any attempt to damage or remove it unless she witnesses the connection herself.

"You cannot stop the show." — Chaelynn, when Magnus urges intervention during Act 1.

Magnus Eillin — Sideshow Storyteller

Who He Is

A traveling storyteller, bard, and sideshow performer. Wears a long blue coat adorned with golden stars. Entertains children with magical flourishes, exaggerated stories, and observations about the stars and the empty spaces between them.

"It belongs in a library."
His Role in Phase Two

Magnus is perceptive — more than he lets on. During Act 1 of the Grand Performance, he pulls the party aside quietly and points them toward the artifact. He has noticed something is wrong with it but cannot articulate exactly what.

He will not confront Chaelynn directly. He knows she will not stop the show. But he believes the party might be able to do something about it — if they move quickly.

Story Role — Pre-Zarathrax

This version of Magnus predates the larger Zarathrax storyline. He is here to establish warmth, character, and the feeling of a living world. Protect his role as an emotional anchor before the horror begins.

The Artifact — Investigation & Truth
Recent modification · What's inside · How to stop it
▼

What It Appears To Be

The Centerpiece

A crystalline lantern, mirrored astrolabe, or ornate "guiding star" at the center of the carnival. It has been the carnival's most prized possession for years — and for years it appeared to be nothing more than a beautiful, mildly magical object that made performances feel more vivid and audiences more engaged.

It caused no harm. No one suffered. The carnival flourished.

Something changed recently. The artifact itself did not — but something was done to it.

Investigation — What They Find

Arcana / Investigation — DC 14

The artifact has been modified. The external casing shows faint tool marks and residue from a recent opening — it has been taken apart and reassembled within the last few months. The magical signature around the seams is slightly misaligned, like a clock that has been wound too tightly.

Arcana / Investigation — DC 17

Something inside the artifact is doing the actual work. The artifact itself is a conduit — an amplifier — but the parasitic intelligence driving the feeding cycle was inserted into it. The original artifact was genuinely benign. Whatever is inside it now is using it as a shell.

If Carefully Disassembled

Inside the artifact is a secondary object — a seed, a gem, a coiled thing — that does not belong. It pulses faintly. It is warm. It reacts to proximity by growing slightly brighter.

This is the true source. The artifact is innocent. The inserted object is not.

The Key Question

Why Did It Only Start Recently?

The artifact has been with the carnival for years without causing harm. The pallor, the insects, the feeding cycle — all of these are recent. A sharp party will notice this and ask why.

The answer: the artifact was modified. Someone opened it, inserted something inside, and resealed it. The modification is subtle but detectable on close examination.

  • Who did this — and when — is a thread for further investigation
  • It was done recently enough that the convoy disruptions and the artifact modification may be connected
  • The same faction engineering regional instability may have also seeded the carnival
The artifact is a weapon that was pointed at the carnival from the outside.

How To Stop It

Break the Artifact

Fastest. If the artifact is destroyed at any point during the Grand Performance — including during the Nightbloom — all creatures immediately die. The feeding cycle collapses instantly.

Violent magical backlash. May damage the surrounding area.
Remove or Destroy the Insert

If the party can disassemble the artifact carefully, they can remove the inserted object and destroy it separately. This ends the feeding cycle and preserves the original artifact — which is genuinely valuable and not evil.

Sever the Resonance

Disrupt the music, lantern patterns, magical harmonics, or emotional amplification. This does not destroy the source but breaks the feeding cycle for the night — a temporary solution.

  • Establishes trust with Chaelynn and carnival characters
  • Introduces combat via kobolds and tunnel encounters

Phase One Resolution

The party recovers the convoy and restores the carnival's supplies. This victory is real and earned. The carnival can finally reopen fully.

And in doing so, the party unknowingly enables Phase Two.

The Insects

Behavior & Presence
  • Moths, beetles, thin-bodied crawlers, parasitic entities
  • Gather near sleeping areas, shaded wagons, lanterns, emotionally charged spaces
  • Drawn toward people and darkness — not food
  • Behave unnaturally: geometric clustering, unnatural stillness
  • Do not burn when touching open flame near the artifact
  • In late stage: move beneath afflicted skin

The Pallor — Key GM Notes

Why Is This Happening Now?

The artifact is not new. The pallor is. A perceptive party should notice this contradiction and ask why the symptoms are recent.

The answer connects to the artifact's modification — something was inserted into it recently, and the pallor has been worsening since. Workers who spend the most time near the artifact are the most afflicted.

The Creatures — If The Outbreak Occurs

What Emerges
  • Malformed, newly born, insectoid
  • Wet, screaming, unstable — not elegant monsters
  • Something unfinished. Something wrong.
  • Three hosts: the Nightbloom performer + two workers in the crowd
If the artifact is destroyed at any point, all creatures die instantly.
Encounter Objectives
  • Protect civilians from panicking crowd
  • Prevent further panic (which feeds the artifact if intact)
  • Fight the emerged creatures
  • Destroy or sever the artifact to end the cycle

Resolution Paths

Destroy the Artifact

Fastest. All creatures die immediately. Violent magical backlash — may damage part of the carnival. Definitive but costly. Chaelynn will grieve the artifact but understand.

Remove the Insert

If the party disassembled the artifact earlier, they can destroy the inserted object specifically. This ends the cycle and preserves the original artifact — which is genuinely valuable and was never evil. The cleanest ending.

Sever the Resonance

Disrupt the music, lanterns, or emotional amplification. Breaks the feeding cycle for the night but does not destroy the source. A temporary fix that buys time for a permanent solution.

Aftermath

Chaelynn

Whatever happens to the artifact, Chaelynn's reaction should reflect what the party has shown her. If they were honest with her, she is shaken but grateful. If they destroyed the artifact without explanation, she is devastated and furious before she understands.

She will want to know who modified the artifact — and why. This thread points outward toward the larger regional conspiracy.

The Larger Thread

The same faction engineering regional instability between Elkenquay and Fastingbreak may have seeded the carnival with the artifact modification. The convoy disruption and the artifact modification may share a single origin. This is a thread for further investigation beyond Hollowgate.

Shared Threads

The Tunnel Connection

The kobold tunnels that diverted the carnival's convoys are the same tunnels Eric traced. His X marks align with tunnel entrances near the city. His final note confirms kobolds and "someone paying them." The carnival's missing convoys are part of the same diversion operation.

The 400gp Reward

Jeffrey offered 400gp for the return of the latest missing convoy. Recovering the carnival's supplies and recovering the convoy for Jeffrey may be the same objective — or closely linked ones that reward the party twice for the same investigation.

Complications

Vanya's Mission — Still Active

Vanya's faction briefing refers to "the current gate scribe" as the target — not Eric. If the party is investigating the carnival arc, Vanya's mission is still running in parallel. Her target is still alive. The carnival arc may bring both threads to a head simultaneously.

Who Modified the Artifact?

The artifact was modified recently — within the last few months. The same window as the convoy disruptions. Whether the artifact modification and the convoy diversion were carried out by the same actor is an open question — and answering it points toward the larger conspiracy driving the Elkenquay/Fastingbreak tensions.

Phase One — The False Horror

The Velvet Husk

A performer whose transformation completed incorrectly. Fast-moving skirmisher. Flickers in dim light, teleports through shadows, pressures isolated targets. The party's first instinct is to focus on this — it is the most visible threat.

The Gorged Lanternling

A bloated larval feeder swollen with emotional energy. Slow at first, then frantic below half HP. Area denial, crowd disruption, and a Death Burst that triggers the phase transition when it dies.

When either creature dies — the artifact pulses. The Broodmother tears into the world.

Phase Two — The True Horror

The Lantern Broodmother

The true source of the pallor. Battlefield controller and emotional parasite. Feeds on frightened creatures, spawns mites, drags targets into bite range. Slow but sustained. The crowd's panic literally heals it.

Pallid Mites

Three appear at transition. More can be spawned. Individually trivial — in groups they stack disadvantage on Wisdom saves, compounding every other threat on the battlefield.

The Nuclear Option

Destroy the Artifact

At any point during the encounter — all creatures die instantly. The party may race for it mid-combat if they piece it together. AC 12, HP 15. Magical backlash: DC 12 Dex save, 2d6 force in 10 ft radius.

GM Summary — The Emotional Core

  • The carnival arc works because the players' success causes the horror. This must be earned, not telegraphed.
  • Magnus is the party's early warning — he notices something wrong but cannot stop the show alone. Give him a quiet, urgent moment during Act 1.
  • The artifact is innocent. What was done to it is not. This distinction matters for Chaelynn and for the larger conspiracy.
  • The kobold tunnels tie Phase One directly to the Hollowgate investigation. Same conspiracy, two different faces.
  • Phase Two should feel like a betrayal of the joy the players helped create. That is the point.
