The Blackglass Laboratory

Act 2 Encounter  ·  Viktor Voss & The Resonance Engine  ·  Level 5–7 Party

"Nothing here was built by madness. That is what makes it horrifying."

Encounter Overview

Setting & Tone

The party descends into the ruins of Dunnshire and finds the Blackglass Laboratory beneath — a hidden research facility that is clean, clinical, industrial, and terrifyingly organized. Steam hisses through copper-veined conduits. Mechanical arms twitch overhead. Somewhere in the dark, restrained things breathe.

"The air smells of antiseptic fluid, burned metal, and blood. Everything is cataloged. Everything is intentional."
Encounter Design Intent
  • Level 5–7 party; three escalating phases
  • The players' emotional responses are the primary mechanic — the room feeds on them
  • Positive emotional resonance (inspiration, triumph) is more powerful than suffering — this should surprise both Voss and the party
  • Voss should be encountered as a scientist, not a screaming villain — he observes, annotates, corrects people who call him a necromancer
  • The laboratory itself is the final boss as much as Voss is
The Retroactive Reveal

Somewhere in Phase 2 or 3, a player should be able to piece together what this place is. The cavehoppers in the Prologue tunnels. The Pallor Brood at the carnival. The creature in the sarcophagus in Dunmore. The resonance gems they have been carrying since Session 1.

All of it traces back here. To this room. To this man.

Let that land without commentary. Don't underline it.
Environmental Interaction
  • Destroying resonance pylons reduces active gem count and may trigger local discharges
  • Containment tanks can be ruptured — releasing subjects or flooding the floor
  • Surgical rigs and mechanical arms are lair action hazards but can also be turned against Voss
  • The Resonance Engine at the center of the chamber is targetable — each hit charges the nearest gem
  • Disconnecting Voss's third arm removes his bonus action utility
Core Mechanic — Critical Hit Absorption

Critical hits do not deal bonus damage inside the Blackglass Laboratory. Instead, attacks deal normal damage and the excess emotional surge is absorbed into the nearest resonance gem, immediately adding one charge to it.

Visually: the energy ripples away from the wound and traces through glowing conduits in the floor toward the nearest pylon. This is the first signal to players that something is fundamentally wrong with this space.

This applies to both player and creature critical hits. The laboratory feeds on everyone.

The Resonance Gem System

Six emotional states charge corresponding gems embedded throughout the laboratory. Each gem tracks charges independently. At 3 charges, a gem discharges — altering the battlefield, empowering creatures, or imposing conditions. The room reacts continuously to emotional intensity. Players should begin noticing the pattern before they understand the mechanic.

Fear Resonance
Triggers

Fear spells · Terror effects · Panic conditions · Frightened status applied to any creature

Build-Up Cues

Lights dim slightly · Low-frequency hum deepens · Shadows lengthen at the edges of the room · Restrained creatures begin twitching violently

Discharge — Terror Pulse

30 ft radius. WIS Save DC 15. Failure: frightened until end of next turn + 2d6 psychic damage. All hidden or dormant creatures awaken. Area lighting extinguishes. Fear effect spreads to adjacent uncharged creatures.

Rage Resonance
Triggers

Barbarian Rage · Reckless Attack · Multiattack flurries · Frenzied aggression · Critical hits (via absorption)

Build-Up Cues

Rhythmic heartbeat-like pounding from the Engine · Conduit tubing overheats to visible red · Machinery vibrates violently at the joints

Discharge — Berserker Cascade

All creatures gain +2 melee damage and +10 movement, but cannot voluntarily disengage. Containment restraints rupture. Caged creatures mutate aggressively and enter combat. Containment chambers crack open.

Pain Resonance
Triggers

Critical hits against the party · Any creature reduced below half HP · Massive single-hit damage (20+ in one attack)

Build-Up Cues

Sharp metallic ringing that rises in pitch · Fluid systems begin cycling faster, audible through the walls · The Engine's central chamber brightens

Discharge — Regenerative Burst

Voss heals 3d8 HP. All laboratory creatures heal 2d6 HP. Severed tissue reconnects. Inactive experimental subjects reactivate on their next initiative count.

Grief Resonance
Triggers

Critical failures · Ally incapacitation or death · Expressions of despair or surrender

Build-Up Cues

Whispering sounds from the walls — indistinct, almost familiar · A sense of emotional weight settling over the room · Hallucinatory echoes at the edge of vision

Discharge — Echo Manifestation

WIS Save DC 15. Failure: disadvantage on attacks and checks + 2d6 psychic damage. Emotional hallucinations manifest. Spectral echoes of failed experiments twitch to life and move to intercept. Duration: until end of next round.

Triumph Resonance
Triggers

Killing laboratory creatures · Destroying Voss's creations · Decisive heroic victories · Destroying resonance pylons

Build-Up Cues

Violent arcs of energy leap between pylons · Structural instability — dust falls from the ceiling · Gems glow with unstable, blinding brightness

Discharge — Catastrophic Overload

DEX Save DC 16. Failure: 6d6 force/fire/lightning damage. Half on success. Structural collapse begins. Random surges trigger each subsequent round. Creature control systems fail. Chain reactions spread to adjacent gems. Voss realizes triumph resonance is stronger than suffering.

Inspiration Resonance
Triggers

Bardic Inspiration · Rally effects · Heroic speeches · Any ability that grants advantage through courage

Build-Up Cues

Harmonic tones that almost resolve into a chord · Machinery stabilizes and rhythmically pulses · Steam releases evenly rather than violently

Discharge — Harmonic Resonance Wave

All negative emotional conditions on party members are cleansed. Players who successfully hit this round gain +2 on attacks next round. Monsters automatically stabilize — fear and frenzy suppressed. Mutation instability pauses. This deeply unsettles Voss. He begins realizing positive resonance is more stable than suffering.

Voss tracks resonance charges openly on his turn — he narrates build-up cues as observational data. Players should hear him and gradually understand what he is measuring.
Lair Actions — Initiative Count 20

On initiative count 20 (losing ties), the laboratory itself acts. Voss does not control these directly — they are the facility responding to accumulated resonance. Choose one each round, or roll randomly if the energy state warrants chaos.

Steam Vent Rupture. A burst of pressurized steam erupts from a ruptured conduit in a 10-foot line. DEX Save DC 15. Failure: 3d6 fire damage and blinded until end of next turn.
Resonance Arc. Two arcs of gem energy leap to separate targets within 40 feet. Each: 2d8 lightning + 2d8 psychic damage. No save — the laboratory does not warn you.
Containment Failure. One containment chamber cracks open, releasing a Skitterling, Stitchman Prototype, or (at Phase 3) a Resonance Brute or Tendril Mass. Roll or choose based on pacing.
Surgical Rig Activation. A mechanical arm descends toward a target within 20 feet. +6 to hit. 2d6 piercing damage. On hit: target is restrained (escape DC 14 STR) as the rig attempts to pin them to a surface for examination.

Phase One — Clinical Observation

Doctor Viktor Voss Enters. The Engine Is Stable.

He is not panicking. He is studying you. The room is mostly quiet. The gems pulse faintly.
"He steps out of the surgical theater without hurry. No dramatic entrance. He looks at each of you the way a physician looks at a patient — not with malice, but with assessment. He picks up a clipboard. He begins writing. 'Interesting,' he says. 'You made it further than the last group.'"

Phase One is about establishing the encounter's wrongness before combat escalates. Voss fights methodically. He does not overextend. He observes. If a player shouts something emotional — a threat, a declaration, a desperate appeal — he notes it. The resonance gems respond to the players before they understand why.

Running Voss — Combat Notes
  • He is a scientist first. Voss narrates observations on his turn — "Rage resonance, three charges. Interesting threshold." He should never sound rattled in Phase 1.
  • Open with Injector Lance targeting whoever looks most physically capable — Hemorrhage Compound on the fighter, Paralytic Serum on anyone who charged immediately.
  • Use Resonance Marker on the party's most emotionally expressive character — the one most likely to inspire or rage.
  • Conductive Hook to pull mobile characters into surgical rig range where the lair action can restrain them. Never chase — let the rigs do the repositioning.
  • Save Resonance Extraction for when a character is already emotionally compromised. The additional damage on frightened/enraged targets is significant.
  • Phase 2 shift (below 60 HP or when first gem discharges): Voss begins moving. He stops merely observing. "I need a better angle." He sounds more interested, not more afraid.
  • Phase 3 shift (below 30 HP or when Triumph resonance fires): The first genuine crack. He looks at the Engine. He looks at the party. "That is… not what I predicted." He injects himself with Hyperoxygenation Burst. He is no longer calm — he is desperate to understand what is happening before the room kills everyone.

Running Morrow
  • Morrow begins the encounter on the ceiling — players may not notice it immediately. It descends when Voss is first attacked, not before.
  • It is not aggressive by default. It is watchful. This should produce a moment of horrible recognition for anyone who fought kobold cavehoppers in Hollowgate — the body plan is unmistakable.
  • Prioritize dragging targets away from Voss. Its tactical role is separation — pull a character into an isolated corner and keep them pinned with Tendon Hook while Morrow works on them.
  • Loyalty Response at 30 HP is a genuine roleplaying beat. Morrow abandons its target mid-combat to protect Voss. Some players may hesitate to kill it. That hesitation is intentional.
  • If Inspiration resonance discharges while Morrow is in a frenzy, it visibly calms. It may stop attacking entirely and move next to Voss. Voss will note this in his annotations with visible disturbance.
Phase Transition — First Gem Discharges

The Engine Responds

The first gem discharge marks the transition to Phase 2. Whichever resonance fires first — the aftermath reshapes the room. Containment chambers begin showing stress fractures. Fluid systems cycle erratically. Voss stops annotating for a moment and looks directly at the Engine.

He says: "Charge rate is ahead of projection. Note that." Then he keeps fighting.

He is not afraid. He is delighted. That distinction matters.
⁂
Phase Two — Escalation

The Laboratory Becomes Unstable. Containment Begins to Fail.

Voss grows increasingly fascinated. He begins taking notes mid-combat. Additional creatures enter from ruptured chambers.

In Phase 2, the encounter widens. Containment failures introduce additional creatures into the fight — Skitterlings first, then Stitchman Prototypes if the combat extends. Voss himself becomes more mobile and more aggressive, but he continues narrating. His tone shifts from clinical to something closer to excitement. He is watching the Engine do things he has not observed before.

Phase 2 Escalation — Containment Failure Schedule

Each round of Phase 2, roll a d6 at initiative count 20 after the lair action. On a 4+, one additional containment chamber cracks open in addition to the normal lair action. Use this order unless pacing demands otherwise:

  • Round 1 of Phase 2: Two Skitterlings drop from ceiling vents
  • Round 2: One Stitchman Prototype pushes through a cracked chamber door
  • Round 3+: GM discretion — Resonance Brute if gems are charged; Tendril Mass if grief/pain resonance dominates

Do not flood the encounter. The escalation should feel like pressure building, not a swarm. Two to three additional creatures in play at any time is the ceiling.

Phase Transition — Triumph or Inspiration Resonance Discharges

The Engine Overloads

When Triumph or Inspiration resonance first reaches full discharge, the Engine destabilizes beyond Voss's projections. The room shakes. Two containment tanks rupture simultaneously. The Engine's central chamber pulses with blinding light.

Voss looks at his notes. Then at the party. "That is not— " He stops. "Positive resonance output is exceeding projections by a factor of— " He stops again. He looks genuinely lost for the first time.

This is the moment the players see it. He built a machine to weaponize suffering. And what's breaking it is courage.
⁂
Phase Three — Overload

The Laboratory Is a Living Emotional Reactor. Leave or Finish It.

Structural collapse begins. Every round without resolution adds new hazards. Voss injects raw resonance directly into himself.

Phase 3 is a clock. Structural collapse is actively occurring — each round, one new environmental hazard activates automatically. The party must choose: finish Voss now, or the room kills everyone.

Phase 3 — Structural Collapse (Each Round, Initiative Count 20)

In addition to the normal lair action, one structural event occurs automatically each round. Roll d6 or choose based on dramatic need:

  • 1–2: A section of ceiling collapses — 15 ft area becomes difficult terrain; creatures in the area DEX Save DC 14 or 2d10 bludgeoning
  • 3–4: A resonance conduit ruptures — 20 ft line of energy, 3d6 force damage, no save
  • 5: Preservation tank detonates — 10 ft radius acid/chemical splash, 2d8 acid, DEX Save DC 14 for half
  • 6: The Engine pulses — all charged gems discharge simultaneously
Voss at Phase 3 — Raw Resonance Injection

When Voss first drops below 30 HP in Phase 3, he injects raw resonance fluid directly from the Engine's tubing into his own bloodstream. This triggers Hyperoxygenation Burst automatically (regardless of recharge). He takes one additional action immediately. He also gains the Resonance Saturation trait for the rest of the encounter: whenever a gem discharges, he heals 1d8 HP and his next attack deals +2d6 resonance damage.

He is trying to understand what is happening. The injection is not desperation — it is data collection, conducted on himself, in real time.

Aftermath — What Remains

  • If Voss is killed, the Engine begins a controlled shutdown — gems dim sequentially. The structural collapse slows but does not reverse. The party has roughly ten minutes to leave before critical sections fail completely.
  • If Voss is incapacitated or surrenders, he will answer questions. Calmly. He will name Percival Malden without hesitation — not as a betrayal, but because he has no loyalty to Percival and sees no reason to protect him. "He was a sponsor. He funded the work. That is the sum of our arrangement."
  • Morrow — if Voss is dead — does not flee and does not attack. It moves to Voss's body and stays there. What the party does with this information is theirs to decide.
  • The resonance gems scattered throughout the laboratory can be recovered. They now carry the emotional charge of the entire encounter — powerful, unstable, and deeply significant to whatever comes next.
  • Voss's records are intact — meticulously organized, fully legible, and contain Percival's name, funding amounts, acquisition orders, and a complete list of deployed monstrosities going back years. Everything the party has fought is in those files. Everything traces back to Percival.
